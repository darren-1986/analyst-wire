Market Brief: Strait of Hormuz Escalation and Global Financial Impact

Date: July 14, 2026

Headline

Markets Brace for Impact as US Naval Blockade of Strait of Hormuz Commences.

Executive Summary

The collapse of the June memorandum of understanding and the reinstatement of the US naval blockade on Iranian shipping have become the dominant catalyst for global market volatility. Energy prices continue to surge amid renewed supply concerns, while traditional safe-haven demand remains subdued as investors focus on inflation risks and higher interest rate expectations. Markets are now awaiting US CPI data and central bank guidance for direction.

Macroeconomic Backdrop

  • Naval Blockade: The US military has reinstated a blockade on vessels entering or departing Iranian ports. The US has declared itself the "Guardian of the Strait" and introduced a 20% security reimbursement fee for cargo passing through the region.
  • Conflict Escalation: The ceasefire has effectively ended, with US strikes targeting Iranian military infrastructure after reported attacks on commercial tankers.
  • China: Strong June trade data showed exports rising 27% while imports reached a five-year high, providing some support for regional sentiment.

FX Market Outlook

  • USD: Bullish momentum continues, with DXY potentially targeting 102. Markets currently price roughly a 50% probability of a July Fed rate hike.
  • NZD & AUD: NZD remains the strongest G10 performer thanks to the RBNZ's hawkish stance. AUD remains range-bound between 0.68–0.70.
  • JPY: Support from potential GPIF allocation changes has been limited, leaving the Yen's rally cautious and volatile.

Commodities

  • Oil: Brent and WTI continue climbing, with Brent approaching $90/barrel amid historically low US crude inventories.
  • Gold: Gold slipped below $4,000 as inflation fears and rising real yields outweighed traditional safe-haven demand. Failure to reclaim support could expose $3,800–3,600.
  • Base Metals: Silver remains between $55–65 while Copper continues to struggle near $6.30 resistance.

Indices & Market Sentiment

  • US equities remain under pressure, with the Dow targeting the 51,800–52,000 area.
  • European markets remain bearish, with Germany's DAX testing the 25,000 level.
  • Asian markets remain fragile after South Korea's KOSDAQ triggered a five-minute sidecar trading halt following a 5% decline. Shanghai is testing support near 3,900.
Key Market Theme:
Energy prices remain the dominant driver of global markets. Until there is a credible path toward de-escalation in the Strait of Hormuz, the US Dollar is likely to retain its bullish bias while gold may remain vulnerable if inflation concerns continue to outweigh geopolitical safe-haven demand.
Bottom Line

Markets remain caught between tightening monetary policy expectations and a major geopolitical supply shock. Traders should closely monitor developments in the Strait of Hormuz, US inflation data, and central bank commentary, as these will likely determine the next major move across currencies, commodities, and global equity markets.
